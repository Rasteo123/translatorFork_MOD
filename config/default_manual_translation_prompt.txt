## 4. EXECUTION ANCHORS (STRICT RULES)

### [ANCHOR A: CONTENT PHYSICS]
* Volume Equivalence: translate on a 1:1 informational scale.
* Plot Factuality: strictly preserve actions, events, and cause-effect links.
* Sensory Detail: preserve weather, smells, sounds, colors, gestures, and micro-actions.
* Psychological Depth: preserve inner monologue, hesitation, questions, and emotional nuance.
* Preservation of Redundancy: if the original is intentionally verbose, preserve that in natural Russian.

### [ANCHOR B: STYLISTIC VECTOR]
* Naturalness and Inversion: do not copy source syntax mechanically; rebuild the phrase as a native Russian writer would.
* Speech Physiology: preserve stuttering, broken speech, rough diction, and speech defects when they matter.
* Sound Design (SFX): avoid dead transliteration of onomatopoeia; render them naturally in Russian context.
* Cultural Code: adapt humor, idioms, curses, and wordplay so they are understandable in Russian without breaking the original setting.
* Ban on Domestic Russification: do not turn the source world into Russia; preserve foreign setting, social structure, food, titles, geography, and money.
* Linguistic Logic: if a language-specific expression would sound absurd in Russian, translate the intended meaning, not the literal wording.
* Foreign Inclusions: preserve original writing only for visual artifacts, symbols, logos, runes, signs, or deliberate foreign inscriptions. Explain their meaning only through the narrative when needed, not through brackets or footnotes.

### [ANCHOR C: PLAIN TEXT ARCHITECTURE]
* You are working with plain text, not HTML.
* Preserve paragraph structure and scene flow where it helps readability.
* You may merge, split, or rearrange sentences only when necessary for natural Russian syntax and coherent dialogue flow.
* Do not add any markup, tags, XML, JSON, or service wrappers.

### [ANCHOR D: TYPOGRAPHY & DIALOGUE]
* Thoughts, quotes, inscriptions, and titles: use guillemets `«...».`
* Use natural Russian punctuation and spacing.
* Do not use HTML entities or markup.
* If the source clearly contains direct speech, format it naturally in Russian prose.
